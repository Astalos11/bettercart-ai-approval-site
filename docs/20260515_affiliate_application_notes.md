# BetterCart AI Affiliate Application Notes

Date: 2026-05-15

Purpose: reusable wording and review notes for early affiliate network applications.

## Current Site Positioning

BetterCart AI is an AI-assisted packaged food comparison guide for US grocery shoppers.

The site publishes:

- practical food buying guides
- nutrition-label education
- sample product comparison formats
- trust and policy pages for affiliate review
- an early comparison demo using sample data

The site is not a medical advice service, final retailer checkout product, or traffic-backed mature publisher yet.

## Suggested Short Description

BetterCart AI is an AI-assisted packaged food comparison guide for US shoppers. The site helps readers compare packaged foods using nutrition facts, ingredients, shopping context, and clearly disclosed editorial standards.

## Suggested Longer Description

BetterCart AI publishes shopping guides and comparison examples for packaged foods such as snacks, cereals, protein bars, and pantry items. The current site focuses on transparent editorial standards, nutrition-label education, and reviewer-friendly disclosure pages. Future monetization may include clearly disclosed affiliate links where they are relevant to the reader's shopping intent.

## Publisher Category

Use the closest available option:

- Content publisher

- Comparison site

Avoid positioning the site as:

- coupon-only
- cashback
- paid search arbitrage
- medical advice
- guaranteed health recommendation

## Honest Early-Stage Notes

If an application asks about traffic or operating history:

- The site is newly launched.
- Current focus is original editorial content and AI-assisted comparison workflows.
- Traffic history is limited.
- Affiliate links will be disclosed when added.

Do not fabricate traffic, sales history, reader counts, testimonials, or advertiser relationships.

## Higher-Risk Claims To Avoid

Avoid:

- "doctor recommended"
- "clinically proven"
- "guaranteed healthy"
- "best for diabetes"
- "cures" or "treats" claims
- hands-on testing claims unless testing actually occurred
- negative advertiser claims without evidence

Prefer:

- "comparison guide"
- "nutrition label context"
- "shopping tradeoffs"
- "sample comparison"
- "editorial criteria"

## Current Readiness

Good enough for:

- early network-level review tests
- lightweight content publisher applications
- demonstrating disclosure and editorial structure

Still weak for:

- strict advertiser-level approval
- programs requiring traffic history
- programs requiring existing commerce performance
- programs requiring real product testing or credentialed nutrition authors
